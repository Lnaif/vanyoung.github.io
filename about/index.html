What is 乃逸夫

The character 逸 (yì) means free and natural , a belief in the way of life, and 夫 (fū) means a person, after the adjective. The character 乃 (nǎi) in Classical Chinese could be explained be as a judgement word. So 乃逸夫 means:

A man free and natural.

Why Naif

乃逸夫 pronounced as [nǎi yì fū] in Chinese, is very similar to the English word naive[naɪˈi:v].

Naive have a meaning of someone is artless, honest or kind, it’s also an attitude of life.So I just simply replace the suffix -ve with -f, eventually Naif as a noun, could be interpreted as:

An artless,honest and kind person.

Naive also means someone lacks experience and so expect things to be easy. It’s a fatal flaw, especially for me who wanna be a programmer. So, just bear it in mind and raising the standard of knowledge to avoid staying naive.

Slogan

Mouth sealed, cash in.

This slogan comes from a Chinese proverb, which has a long history and widely spreaded in recent years. According to the Baidu Encyclopedia [1], it explained as:

  1. Sometimes do not talk to others or make publicity could better safeguard and protect your own interests.
  2. Do not let others know what you are doing or see your benefits, to avoid letting others jealousy or envy.

In my opinion, is’s original meaning is not teach people to become selfish.Instead, it could be interpreted as:

The real wealthy people would not just say without practicing, but with practical action to prove themselves.

So yes, just make move, no empty words.

That something could be code, or notes. So I just simply named this EXCITED NOTES.
